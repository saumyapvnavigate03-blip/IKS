The Beginning

Ancient Roots, Eternal Wisdom

Ayurveda originated from the sacred Vedic texts of ancient India, particularly the Atharva Veda. Transmitted orally through generations of sages, it was systematized into the great texts we know today — the Charaka Samhita and Sushruta Samhita. It is not merely medicine — it is a complete philosophy of living in harmony with nature.

ॐ
~3000 BCE
The Vedic Origins
Oral transmission of healing knowledge in the Vedic tradition. The Atharva Veda contains the earliest references to medicinal plants and healing practices — passed from sage to student across generations without a single written word.
This oral tradition was deliberate — knowledge was considered sacred, meant only for those prepared to receive it. The precision of transmission across millennia is itself a testament to the civilisation's intellectual depth.
ॐ
~600 BCE
Charaka Samhita — The Internal Medicine Bible
Charaka Samhita compiled by sage Charaka — the foundational text on internal medicine, covering diagnosis, treatment, and preventive care. 8 branches, 120 chapters, 341 medicinal plants documented.
Charaka wrote: "A physician who fails to enter into the body of a patient with the lamp of knowledge and understanding can never treat diseases." — 2600 years before evidence-based medicine existed as a concept.
Charaka Samhita, Sutrasthana 9.3
ॐ
~400 BCE
Sushruta Samhita — The Birth of Surgery
Sushruta Samhita establishes Ayurvedic surgery with over 300 surgical procedures and 120 surgical instruments — including rhinoplasty, cataract surgery, and C-section techniques.
Sushruta's rhinoplasty technique — reconstructing the nose using a flap of forehead skin — was independently "rediscovered" by British surgeons in 1794 and published as new. It was 2200 years old.
Sushruta Samhita, Sutrasthana 16
ॐ
~700 CE
Ashtanga Hridayam — The Eight Branches
Ashtanga Hridayam by Vagbhata synthesizes the classical knowledge into a comprehensive eight-branched system — internal medicine, surgery, ENT, paediatrics, toxicology, psychiatry, rejuvenation, and aphrodisiacs.
A complete medical curriculum 13 centuries before modern medical specialisations emerged. Still studied in Ayurvedic colleges across India today.

Ancient Words · Modern Resonance

What the sages actually wrote.

"A physician who fails to enter into the body of a patient with the lamp of knowledge and understanding can never treat diseases."
Charaka Samhita · Sutrasthana 9.3 · ~600 BCE
Written 2,600 years before evidence-based medicine existed as a concept. Charaka was describing what we now call patient-centred care.
"It is more important to know what sort of person has a disease than to know what sort of disease a person has."
Charaka Samhita · ~600 BCE
Precision medicine — 3000 years early. This statement is now the founding philosophy of the US National Institutes of Health precision medicine initiative (2015).
"Ahara (food) is the best of all medicines. When diet is wrong, medicine is of no use. When diet is correct, medicine is of no need."
Charaka Samhita · ~600 BCE
The first food-as-medicine principle. Now the rapidly expanding field of nutritional psychiatry validates exactly this claim with RCT evidence.
"One should eat only when the previous meal has been digested. This alone protects health."
Ashtanga Hridayam · Vagbhata · ~700 CE
Intermittent fasting — described 1,300 years before its clinical trials. Modern research confirms this exact mechanism through mTOR pathway modulation.
"The life of all living beings is Vata. Without Vata, there is no movement, no function, no life."
Charaka Samhita · Sutrasthana 12.8
Vata's description as the governing force of all biological movement maps almost precisely to the modern understanding of the autonomic nervous system.

Honest Answers

The questions every sceptic asks.

We'll answer them directly. No deflection. No mysticism.

Isn't this just pseudoscience? +
It was — until 2008, when the first peer-reviewed genomic study (Prasher et al., Journal of Translational Medicine) found 251 differentially expressed genes between Vata and Kapha constitutions. It's now an active area of systems biology research at institutions including CSIR-IGIB. The Prakriti phenotype has a confirmed molecular signature. That is not pseudoscience — that is a testable, falsifiable biological finding.
How is this different from astrology or a personality test? +
Astrology makes predictions. Personality tests describe behaviour. Prakriti describes biological constitution — your genomic expression, gut microbiome composition, metabolic rate, and inflammatory baseline. The distinction is testability: Prakriti predictions were made 3000 years ago and when tested with modern genomic tools, they held up. A multimodal AI model trained purely on biological markers independently reproduced the same constitutional classifications. MBTI cannot make that claim.
Can Prakriti change over time? +
Your core Prakriti is fixed at conception — like your blood type. But your Vikruti — your current imbalanced state — changes constantly. Most Ayurvedic treatment targets Vikruti, not Prakriti. This is why your result might not feel like you: you're experiencing your current Vikruti, not your original Prakriti. Most adults are living 30–50% outside their natural state — and that gap is where disease begins.
Is there clinical trial evidence for any of this? +
Yes. Ashwagandha has 12+ randomised controlled trials — including a 2012 double-blind RCT (Chandrasekhar et al.) showing 27.9% cortisol reduction. Brahmi has 6 RCTs on cognitive function. Boswellia for arthritis has 8 RCTs and is used in European clinical practice. Guggulu has lipid-lowering trials comparable to low-dose statins. Curcumin (turmeric) has 100+ clinical trials showing anti-inflammatory effects targeting IL-6 and CRP — the exact biomarkers elevated in Kapha types.
Why wasn't this in my biology textbook? +
Because the peer-reviewed validation is recent (2008–2025), and because there is institutional resistance to acknowledging that a non-Western knowledge system may have anticipated molecular biology by 3000 years. This is a known problem in academia — the systematic undervaluation of indigenous knowledge systems. The data exists. The studies are published. The gap is in acknowledgement, not evidence.
What's the actual accuracy of the Prakriti quiz? +
Self-reported Prakriti questionnaires have 60–75% concordance with expert clinical assessment in validation studies. The AI model using biological data (genomics + microbiome + metabolomics) achieves 83.6% accuracy — higher than self-report. The quiz on this site uses the validated questionnaire framework from peer-reviewed Prakriti assessment tools. Answer based on your most natural, rested state — not your current stressed or imbalanced condition.
